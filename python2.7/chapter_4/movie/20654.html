Stigmata (1999)

reviewed by
Jon Ridge

³ Stigmata ³          *

I missed a 1 o¹clock for ³Stir of Echoes² by six minutes and, not being one to miss a second of the movie-going experience -- I¹m talking lights dimming, previews, even that THX thing with the cows (m¹I psycho, or what) -- I caught a 1:15 of this film, instead. Mistake.

Produced with an MTV-eye, ³Stigmata² is all photography direction. The story, next to impossible to concentrate on one second to the next because the film¹s visual style is so abrasive, follows Atheist hairdresser Frankie Page (Patricia Arquette) as she becomes ³afflicted² with the titular phenomena. Meaning, wounds of Christ; receive five, and you die. So, the film is more or less a countdown to her death. Well.. why is this happening? As a form of extreme punishment on a non-believer, or a method of _making_ them believe? Dunno. Either way, that¹s not entertaining, it¹s depressing. We get loving shots of large nails being hammered into feet and hands, over and over again; divine imagery, right? If it isn¹t Arquette getting slashed or impaled, it¹s blood dripping into water, water dripping into water (Frankie¹s apartment has a convenient leak), or rain -- everything in annoying close-up. Tip: try to make everything look cool (microwaves, candles, coffee cups, more candles.. did I mention water), and nothing does.

As for the gore, I wonder how a movie like this one skirts the NC-17 rating. One scene, featuring an open wound on Arquette¹s wrist that gushes blood so profusely for, like, five seconds, or thereabouts, made me nauseous. That, plus images of carnage scattered throughout at what seemed like two-minute intervals; where¹s the line? A minute¹s worth of sex in greater cinema like ³Eyes Wide Shut² is too obscene for mass audiences, but 100 minutes of bloodshed here isn¹t? Oy... the hypocrisy continues.

If anything, I enjoyed Gabriel Byrne¹s performance as the priest/scientist/whatever the hell he¹s supposed to be. He¹s got a couple reaction takes, off the possessed Frankie, that felt natural. Of course, then they get all doe-eyed toward one another, and flirty, and talk about how he misses sex, and drink beer: how is there time for this? He¹s supposed to be a man of the cloth, what would she see in a -- nevermind.

Bad movie.
