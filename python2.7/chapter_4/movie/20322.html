³ Better Than Chocolate ³          * *

The new les-genda romantic comedy ³Better Than Chocolate² has a good first act, followed by a rather shitty second, then falls apart completely in the third and final. And, with one scene: Maggie (beautiful red-head Karyn Dwyer), who works in an adult novelty shop, has met and fallen for Kim (Christina Cox), a caricature artist; Maggie¹s mother (played by Wendy Crewson) has moved in with them both, unaware the nature of their relationship, and finds them in bed together. She asks what¹s going on, and Maggie lashes out. About feeling controlled by mom, and how their different, and this is her life; blah blah. It¹s a valid argument, actually - or, would be - except that the mother has not once to that moment said a thing negative toward her daughter, or criticized her in any way. So, the scene plays a mite forced and overwrought. Kim, who was waiting for her lover to fess up about them, mopes off with a ³fuck you², and leaves town; contrived. There should be another scene, here, of the girls arguing just a little longer - more than three seconds, I mean - before blowing up at one another and going separate ways. It gets worse: the store where Maggie works has trouble with customs, and in protest, she stands naked on window display for the press. Noone shows up, though, aside from gay-bashers who throw flaries into the store, so what was the point of doing it that time of night? And, what was she hoping to prove? Maggie hasn¹t shown much concern, one way or another, for the whole censorship issue (save for a book ice sculpture, don¹t ask), nor the store¹s welfare. So, um... why? Channeling a bit of emotionally-distraught energy through anything but pining futilly over lost love? I don¹t know; unclear. And, why do gay-bashers have to make an appearance (twice, no less) at all? We even get an explosion (how very Hollywood, Trimark) in the end to tidy everything up - relationships, everything - and you can just hear that Sarah MacLachlan song coming on, as everybody stands around, happy, and our lovers reunite at the last second. Too much, too much. ³Better² has a distinct s.f.w. air about it, almost from first frame to last. The best thing about it, these two girls, should¹ve been the film¹s focalpoint, but they're treated as an afterthought. Which is a shame, because Cox (who resembles a grown-up twin from ³Full House²) and Dwyer make a nice, sexy couple. They¹re attractive, good- natured and totally into eachother. But, they¹re forgotten; two indisputably erotic sex scenes, and that¹s it. I especially liked the dance club sequence, where the one girl gives the other a rose; and, the body painting. But, it¹s hard to compete with such trite filler: do we care what Maggie¹s dingbat mom (fine to be the comic stooge, but show a little intelligence) will think of her daughter¹s lifestyle? Or, if the drag queen (well played by Peter Outerbridge) will win over the neurotic store owner? Does the film have to tell us not everyone with same-sex proclivity is accepted in today¹s society; duh, we know that, let¹s move on. This is one of the year¹s worst scripts, about so much it¹s about nothing. I¹d keep act one, because the dialogue is smooth and witty, remove everything else that happens in the film, leave the sex where it is and start over. Or, no - just take these two young leads and put them in something smarter and more serious. They were certainly ready for it.
